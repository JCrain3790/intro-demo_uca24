Coding Academy and My Intro to PC

09.23.2024

This website is going to my blog for my progress in Arkansas Coding Academy. Fair warning that my writing style is very stream of conscious, so I might ramble. But I promise there is a point. I'm starting the Fast Track Full Stack for the Fall of 2024. So far, we have learned how to navigate Debian Linux, how to use HTML, and had a test over HTML terms. I have to say, I'm pretty happy with my progress thus far. I've always enjoyed computers and have been fascinated with the innerworkings of them ever since I bought my first PC in 2020.

Before I bought my first PC, I've always been Mac user. We had a family PC at home when I was growing up, but when I turned 18, I got a Mac book before starting college and never looked back. I liked Mac because I was getting in to video, and Mac was considered the computer of choice for video-enthusiasts. I stuck with them for a while, however in 2015, I began to become frustrated with the direction Apple was going with their hardware. Ditching legacy ports, as well as widely used ports like USB-A, was a move that baffled me. That, along with my increasing interest in PC gaming, pushed me in 2020 to buy my own computer.

The idea of building a computer was still quiet scary at the time, so I opted to buy a custom prebuilt from iBUYPOWER. So in September of 2020, I bought this:

  • CPU: AMD Ryzen 3700x
  • GPU: nVidia RTX 3080 10gb
  • RAM: 16gb DDR4 3200hz
  • MOTHERBOARD:Gigabyte Aorus X570 Elite Wi-Fi
